# DJ Set-Building Guide — the Energy Arc

_Companion to `edm_genres_subgenres_detailed.csv` (see the Energy (1-10), DJ Set Placement, BPM and Transition Tip columns). Updated 2026-07-15._

A good set is a **journey**, not a playlist. You control it with three levers: **tempo (BPM), energy (1–10), and key (Camelot)**. The CSV gives all three per genre so you can plan slots before you touch the decks.

## The classic energy arc

```
Energy
 10 |                         __________
  8 |                    ____/          \___
  6 |              _____/                    \____
  4 |        _____/                              \____
  2 |  _____/                                         \___
    |__________________________________________________________
      Warm-up      Build        Peak        Plateau   Wind-down
```

## Slots, and which genres fit (from the Energy + Set Placement columns)

**Warm-up (energy 1–4, ~110–122 BPM)** — set the mood, don't peak too soon.
Ambient (1), Downtempo (3), Organic House (3), Amapiano (4), Deep House (4), early Melodic House.

**Build (energy 4–6, ~120–126 BPM)** — lift steadily, introduce groove.
House (5), Nu Disco (5), Indie Dance (5), Progressive House (5), Afro House (5), Melodic House & Techno (6).

**Peak (energy 7–9, ~126–140 BPM)** — the main room, maximum floor.
Tech House (6–7), Bass House (7), Trance (7), Techno (8), Psy-Trance (8), Big Room / Mainstage (9), Hard Techno (9).

**Hard peak / special moment (energy 8–10)** — use in bursts, not for long.
Drum & Bass (8), Dubstep (8), Hardstyle / Hardcore (9–10).

**Wind-down (energy back to 3–5)** — release the tension, land the plane.
Melodic House, Deep House, Organic House, Downtempo.

## How to program it

1. **Pick the arc** for the slot/room and time you're playing (opening ≠ prime time ≠ closing).
2. **Sort candidate tracks by Energy then BPM** — build a rough running order that climbs, plateaus, then eases.
3. **Check keys** — keep neighbours within the Camelot rules (see `dj_harmonic_mixing_guide.md`); use relative major/minor to shift mood at transitions.
4. **Plan the bridges** — where genre or BPM changes, use the CSV's **Transition Tip** (bass swap, echo-out, loop roll, halftime/double-time, double-drop).
5. **Leave escape hatches** — a breakdown or beatless section is your exit if a mix isn't landing.

## Rules of thumb

- **Move energy ±1–2 at a time.** Jumping from 4 to 9 empties a floor as fast as staying flat does.
- **Raise tempo gradually** (2–4 BPM steps) unless you deliberately use a halftime/double-time bridge.
- **One "moment" per set** — the big double-drop or genre switch hits hardest when it's rare.
- **Read the room** — the arc is a plan, not a cage; the CSV tells you where a track *sits*, the crowd tells you when to use it.
